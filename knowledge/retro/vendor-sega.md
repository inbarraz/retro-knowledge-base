## Platform-Specific Knowledge

### Sega Master System (Model 3010)

#### Overview
- 8-bit game console, competitor to Nintendo NES - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Z80 processor (same as TRS-80, ZX Spectrum) - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Model 3010 is later revision with cost-reduced design - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Uses cartridge slot and card slot for games - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]

#### Common Failure: Voltage Regulator

##### Symptoms
- No power LED when powered on - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- No current draw from power supply - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Console completely dead - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]

##### Diagnosis
- Check voltage regulator output with multimeter - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Should see 5V on output side - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- If 0V output with good input, regulator is dead - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- 7805 style regulator in TO-220 package - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]

##### Repair
- Replace voltage regulator with equivalent 5V 1A part - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- 7805 or modern switching equivalent works - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Use thermal paste or heatsink if running hot - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]

#### Power Supply
- Uses center-positive barrel jack - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Requires 9-10V DC input - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
- Internal regulator steps down to 5V for logic - Source: [iHR4YDJTPrA]
