#### Where Found
- Used in Leading Edge Model D computers - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Mounted in standard drive bay - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]

#### Common Failure Mode
- Stepper motor bearing seizes over time - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Drive spins up but stepper cannot move through all positions - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Symptoms: Drive powers on, spins briefly, tries to seek, then shuts off - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Parking brake solenoid works, but stepper motor is the problem - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]

#### Revival Technique
- Apply bearing oil to stepper motor shaft - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Exercise stepper manually through stuck positions - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Power cycle repeatedly while working oil into bearing - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- See techniques.md for detailed procedure - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]

#### Important Caveats
- Oil fix is NOT permanent - only for data recovery - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Drive will fail again, especially after storage - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Copy all data off immediately after revival - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
- Proper fix requires replacing stepper motor or bearings - Source: [dErXDjMJ2dA]
