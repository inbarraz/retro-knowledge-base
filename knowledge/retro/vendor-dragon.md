## Platform-Specific Knowledge

### Dragon 32

#### Overview
- Home computer from Dragon Data, Wales, UK - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Made in Great Britain - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Uses same Motorola chipset as TRS-80 Color Computer - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Essentially a CoCo clone with slight differences - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Keyboard layout exactly matches CoCo - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Cannot directly run CoCo software without minor modifications - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]

#### Hardware Quality
- Much more substantial feeling than CoCo - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Superior keyboard compared to TRS-80 CoCo 1 and 2 - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- CoCo 1 had terrible keyboard, CoCo 2 slightly better - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Later CoCo 2 revision improved keyboard, but Dragon still better - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]

#### Power Supply
- PAL version uses external AC transformer - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- External supply outputs 8.5V AC and 14V AC (2x 14V lines) - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Internal power supply converts AC to DC rails - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Likely requires 5V, 12V, and -5V internally - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- UK power supply looks homemade but is original - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- 220V mains input on original PSU - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]

#### Connectors
- 9-pin monitor connection - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- PAL video output (UK/European version) - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- NTSC version was sold in US - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]

#### Repair Notes
- Can replace internal power supply for 120V operation - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Easier than finding voltage converter - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]

#### Historical Note
- Dragon Data lifted heavily from CoCo design - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Too many similarities to be coincidence - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Architecture straight from Motorola chipset reference design - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]

### Software Compatibility
- Astro Blast cartridge exists for Dragon 32 - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
- Game cartridges available specifically for Dragon - Source: [DXOP0uP5P60]
