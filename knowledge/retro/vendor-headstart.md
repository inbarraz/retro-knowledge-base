## Platform-Specific Knowledge

### HeadStart Explorer (EX-938-CP)

#### Overview
- All-in-one PC with integrated keyboard - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Made by Vendex (sold under HeadStart brand) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Entry-level home computer with built-in ROM OS - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Keyboard folds up to cover top of unit - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- No built-in screen despite design suggesting one - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Very heavy for its size - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Built-in ROM Operating System
- Graphical OS boots directly from ROM - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Similar to Tandy DeskMate or GEOS - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Includes word processor, file cabinet (database), calendar, phone book - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Can drop to MS-DOS 3.31 command prompt - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- ROM drive appears as C: with zero bytes free - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Spell check requires floppy disk (not in ROM) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Word processor is basic - no text selection, only cursor movement - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- File cabinet app is primitive HyperCard-like database - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Graphics somewhat slow on 8MHz XT-class system - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Graphics

##### CGA Mode
- Supports 4-color 640x200 mode (more than standard CGA's 2-color) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Similar to Tandy 4-color high-res mode - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Color/Mono switch on rear panel - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Graphics emulation options: CGA or HGC (Hercules) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

##### Video Output
- 9-pin CGA video connector - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- RGB2HDMI compatible with IBM CGA profile - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Compatible with Commodore 1084 monitor - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- NOT VGA - requires CGA monitor - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Expansion
- Single 8-bit ISA slot under removable cover - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Second internal connector for modem/expansion - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Only accepts short ISA cards due to space constraints - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Sound card would fit but uses only slot - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- XT-IDE would work for hard drive replacement - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Cannot run both XT-IDE and sound card simultaneously (single slot) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Ports and Connectors
- Full-height 5.25" floppy drive - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Proprietary bus mouse connector (same as Macintosh serial style) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Microsoft bus mice may be compatible - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Joystick port (standard 9-pin) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Serial port labeled "Modem" - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Printer port - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- External floppy connector labeled "DISC" - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Built-in power supply with standard IEC cord - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Known Issues

##### CMOS Battery
- Lithium battery similar to Macintosh style - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Located under removable cover near ISA slot - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Can be replaced with CR2032 soldered to original connectors - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Settings won't save without working battery - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

##### Hard Drive
- Original hard drive likely MFM type - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- May require oil on stepper motor if making grinding noises - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Percussive maintenance can sometimes spin up stuck drive - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- XT-IDE or SD/CF adapter can replace failed drive - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

##### Power Switch
- Button may be missing from power switch - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Must push recessed switch with finger - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

##### Keyboard
- Big Enter key not properly stabilized - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Works if pressed in center, not on edges - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Other keys are properly stabilized - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Generally mushy feel - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Front Panel LEDs
- Power LED - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Turbo LED - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Floppy drive activity - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Hard drive activity - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

### HeadStart VGA Monitor (M888-VC / Samsung CQ4551)

#### Overview
- 13" VGA monitor - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Actually manufactured by Samsung (model CQ4551) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Branded and sold by Vendex/HeadStart - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- No on-screen display - analog controls only - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Controls
- Only brightness and contrast knobs on front - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- V-Size 1, 2, 3 controls on back - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- No horizontal position/hold controls - picture shifts between resolutions - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### Common Issues

##### Frozen Potentiometers
- Front panel controls often frozen solid - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Grease inside pots has solidified with age - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Contact cleaner can free them - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- May need disassembly to access properly - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

##### Picture Dim Until Warmed Up
- Picture may be dim when first powered on - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Brightens as monitor warms up - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- May indicate capacitor issues - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]

#### VGA Compatibility
- Fixed VGA cable with missing pins (compatible with early VGA cards) - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Works with oldest VGA systems - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Handles mode switching properly - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
- Good color purity - Source: [O3FmOONuvvQ]
