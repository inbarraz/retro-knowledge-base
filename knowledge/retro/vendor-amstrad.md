## Platform-Specific Knowledge

### CPC 464

#### Overview
- Z80-based 8-bit computer (4MHz) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- 64KB RAM - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Released 1984 - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Over 2 million sold in Europe - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Never officially sold in North America - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Built-in cassette player for storage - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Distinctive colorful keys on keyboard - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### Video Capabilities
- Multiple video modes - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- High-res: 640x200 in 2 colors - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Low-res: 160x200 in 16 colors - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Color palette of 27 colors (more than 16 at a time selectable) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- RGB output via proprietary connector - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### Sound
- General Instruments AY-8912 sound chip - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Same chip used in later ZX Spectrums - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Square wave based - not as capable as SID chip - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Built-in speaker with volume control - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### Power Supply
- Powered from monitor - no internal power supply - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Monitor plugs into wall, computer plugs into monitor - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- 5V and 12V required (separate barrel jacks) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Can use external power supply adapter to run without monitor - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### Common Issues
- Temperamental power switch - needs wiggling to work - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Very common failure point - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### Variants
- Multiple versions with different logos - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Later versions have red slash through Amstrad logo - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Made in Korea - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Different PCB layouts between versions (ports in different positions) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

### CPC 6128

#### Overview
- Successor to CPC 464 - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- 128KB RAM (double the 464) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Released 1985 (one year after 464) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Built-in 3-inch floppy drive instead of cassette - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- CPM compatible - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Was actually sold in US briefly (first Amstrad product in US market) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### 3-inch Floppy Drive
- Uses 3-inch discs (not 3.5-inch) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Similar to Famicom Disk System disc format - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Flip disc over to access second side - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Uses NEC 765 floppy controller (same as IBM PC) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Double density MFM encoding at 250Kbps - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Drive belt commonly fails - needs replacement - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Hitachi made original drives - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### CPM Support
- Runs CP/M operating system - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Boot CP/M from BASIC: |CPM command - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- CP/M Plus available on system discs - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### Floppy Emulators
- SD floppy emulators work (GoTek compatible interface) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Uses standard 34-pin edge connector - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Motor enable signal same for internal and external drives - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- LoTharek SD interface compatible - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

#### BASIC Commands
- CAT - directory listing - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- |A or |B - switch between drives A: and B: - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- RUN "filename" - run program from disc - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- MODE command changes video modes - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

### CPC 664

#### Overview
- Released between 464 and 6128 - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Same as 464 but with 3-inch floppy drive instead of cassette - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- No colorful keys (plain keyboard) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

### Plus Range (464 Plus, 6128 Plus)

#### Enhancements
- Added hardware sprites and scrolling - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Expanded color palette: 4096 colors available - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- 16 colors for background, 15 for sprites - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Enhanced sound with DMA transfers - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Reduced processor overhead for sound - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

### GX4000

#### Overview
- Game console version of CPC - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

## Video Output

### RGB Connection
- Proprietary connector to monitor - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Can use SCART cable for modern displays - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Works with RetroTINK 5X Pro via SCART/RGB input - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- May need sample rate adjustment (858 samples per line worked) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Default display: blue background with bright yellow text - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

### Monitor Bundle
- Sold bundled with green or color monitor - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- GT65 was green screen monitor - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Made monitors more accessible (no need to buy separately) - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- Launch price included monitor: £199 green, £359 color - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]

## Software Quality

- Graphics often similar to Spectrum despite CPC's better palette - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
- AY sound chip capable of more than most games utilized - Source: [wMOsbiIYeVg]
