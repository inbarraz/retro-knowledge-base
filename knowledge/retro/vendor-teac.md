## Platform-Specific Knowledge

### TEAC FD-55GFR (5.25" High-Density Floppy Drive)

#### Overview
- Standard 1.2MB high-density 5.25" floppy drive - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- One of the most common PC floppy drives - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Very reliable even after decades of storage - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Full-height and half-height variants exist - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]

#### Dual Speed Operation
- Operates at 360 RPM for 1.2MB high-density disks - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Operates at 300 RPM for 360K double-density disks - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Speed controlled automatically via density select signal - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Some models have manual speed override jumper - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]

#### Track Density
- 96 TPI (tracks per inch) for high-density mode - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Can read 48 TPI disks written on 360K drives - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Writing 360K format on HD drive may cause compatibility issues - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]

#### Jumper Configuration
- Uses I/IS/II drive select strapping - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- IS position (drive select 1) standard for PC cable with twist - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Termination resistor network for last drive on cable - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]

### TEAC FD-235HF (3.5" High-Density Floppy Drive)

#### Overview
- Standard 1.44MB 3.5" floppy drive - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Extremely common and reliable - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Good replacement source for any PC needing 3.5" drive - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]
- Many variants with minor differences - Source: [xtHwYQyhkAc]

### TEAC XR-7000 Cassette Data Recorder

#### Overview
- 21-channel instrumentation recorder - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Wide band data recorder - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Frequency response up to 40 kHz - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- FM wideband Group One recording plus high band recording - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- ID information recording capability - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Very heavy (boat anchor weight) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

#### Display
- Plasma display screen - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Dual display modes: bar graph (all channels) or two-channel waveform - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Display may blank out intermittently (fault symptom) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

#### Tape System
- Uses VHS-style tapes (standard video cassettes) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Not standard VHS recording format - different data format - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Linear recording system (different from helical scan VHS) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Variable tape speed control (affects bandwidth) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Speed range from very slow to high speed - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Higher speed = higher bandwidth recording - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

#### Front Panel
- 21 BNC jacks for channel inputs - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Monitor out and memo in BNC jacks - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- System menu button with self-test function - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Tape transport controls (play, rewind, fast forward) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Tape speed adjustment dial - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

#### Rear Panel
- 25 amp DC fuse - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- ID connections - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Printer connection (with protective cap) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Display internal/external switch - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Channel system reset - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Remote trigger input (TTL level) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Option connector - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- GPIB option - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- ID board slot - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- FM amp board slot - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Channel cards (1-21) in rear slot bays - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

#### Internal Architecture
- Modular channel cards (plug-in) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Each channel card has hybrid modules and surface mount components - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Flip-down back panel for card access - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

#### Known Issues
- Display blanks out after few seconds (fault) - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Monitor mode button may cause display to blank - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Eject mechanism may not latch properly without tape - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Transport may make grinding noises during playback - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Some BNC jacks may get crushed during shipping - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

#### Documentation
- Service manuals extremely difficult to find - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- User manuals also very rare - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Very limited online documentation - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Museum of Magnetic Sound Recording has some info - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]

## General Notes

### Instrumentation Recorders
- Used for scientific/industrial data acquisition - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Multiple simultaneous analog channels - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- FM recording allows DC to audio frequency response - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
- Different from consumer audio/video recorders - Source: [nY38YvAMEFg]
