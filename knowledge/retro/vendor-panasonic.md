### Panasonic Omni TV Series (RGB-Compatible)

#### Japanese RGB Connector
- Japanese market TVs have RGB input via special connector - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Different pinout from European SCART - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Pre-made RGB cables available for Japanese TVs - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Can connect CGA computers directly for RGB video - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]

#### Digital RGB Intensity Bit Issue
- Japanese RGB monitors often don't support intensity bit - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- CGA uses 4-bit RGBI (Red, Green, Blue, Intensity) - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Intensity bit creates 16 colors from 8 base colors - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Without intensity: dark yellow appears instead of brown - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- "Brown" in CGA is actually dark yellow (intensity=0) - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Japanese TVs show all 8 base colors but at full brightness - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]

#### Tandy 1000 CGA Testing
- Tandy 1000EX CGA output tested on Panasonic Omni - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Text display works correctly in both 40 and 80 column modes - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Graphics work but colors affected by missing intensity - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Useful as compact CGA monitor alternative - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]

#### Why Use Japanese TV for CGA
- Small form factor compared to dedicated CGA monitors - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- RGB input provides better quality than composite - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Consumer TVs more readily available than CGA monitors - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]
- Good for space-constrained setups - Source: [NHz1e1Wu1iI]

#### Hot Chassis Design (SAFETY WARNING)

- Adding composite input would expose user to shock hazard - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Only RF input is safe due to isolation transformer - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Light weight indicates no transformer = hot chassis - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]

#### CRT Issues

##### Phosphor Burn
- Center dot burn visible on CRT face - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Caused by picture shrinking to dot on power-off - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- No "dot shutdown" circuitry in older sets - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Phosphor burn is behind glass, cannot be cleaned - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]

##### Grid-to-Cathode Short (Fatal Failure)
- Symptoms: blooming, retrace lines visible - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Image flickers/cuts in and out - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Tapping CRT neck causes image changes - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Short pulls cathode to ground = max brightness - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Drive electronics makes weird noises - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Can come and go intermittently - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]

##### Diagnosing CRT Shorts
- CRT tester shows G1-to-cathode short - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- B&W CRT only connects to one channel (red) - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- Remove shorts function may not work - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
- If short won't clear, CRT is junk - Source: [MnRD1khu4Qk]
