## Platform-Specific Knowledge

### Eyoyo EM08C 8" LCD Monitor

#### Overview
- 8" 1024x768 LCD monitor - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Marketed as IPS but actually TN panel (poor off-axis viewing) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- 4:3 aspect ratio - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Matte screen (reduces glare) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- ~$63 USD from AliExpress (Eyoyo official store) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- 12V 1.5A power supply (center positive barrel jack) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- VESA-compatible removable stand with adjustable height - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Built-in stereo speakers (tinny, adequate for testing only) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Inputs
- VGA (DB-15) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- HDMI - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Composite video (RCA yellow) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Composite video (BNC) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- YPbPr component (via VGA connector, broken/misconfigured) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- USB Type-C (possibly 5V 2A power input) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Stereo audio (RCA) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### OSD Menu Features
- Brightness, color, hue, saturation controls - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Language selection (English, Chinese, French, Italian, German, Spanish, Turkish, Russian) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- OSD timeout adjustable - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Volume control (also accessible via +/- buttons) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Display ratio setting (16:9/4:3) - affects internal scaling - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Screen rotation (flip for inverted mounting) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Blue screen on/off - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Crosshair overlay (for microscope use) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- VGA auto-config function - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### VGA Compatibility (GOOD)
- 640x400 at 70Hz: Works - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- 640x350 at 70Hz (EGA): Works - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- 640x480 at 60Hz: Works - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Auto-calibrates on mode change - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Remembers settings for at least 4 different VGA modes - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Scaling uses filtering (smooth, not pixel-perfect) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- DOS text modes work well from Toshiba Satellite laptop - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Composite Video Compatibility (MIXED)
- NTSC: Supported - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- PAL: Supported - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- 240p: NOT supported - treats all input as interlaced - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Interlacing causes visible jitter on 8-bit computers - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- PAL jitter more noticeable (25fps vs 30fps) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Apple IIc: Works, readable but soft due to interlace artifacts - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Commodore 64 NTSC: Works, colors good - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Commodore 64 PAL: Works, Easy Flash 3 compatible - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- PlayStation: Works for composite video - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- No sharpness control available - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### HDMI Compatibility (GOOD)
- 480p: Works - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- 640x480 over HDMI: Not listed in manual but may work - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- RetroTink 2X line-doubled output: Works well - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- RetroTink 2X pass-through (480i): Does NOT work (black screen) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### VGA via HDMI-to-VGA Adapter (FAILS)
- RetroTink 2X through HDMI-to-VGA adapter: Black screen - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Same setup works on Asus monitor - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Possible timing incompatibility - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Component YPbPr (BROKEN)
- Uses VGA connector for component input - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Appears miswired or misconfigured - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Wrong colors displayed (red text issues) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Only getting signal on red wire, not green - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Tested with Sony Blu-ray player: Does not work properly - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Sync-on-Green (NOT SUPPORTED)
- Mac IIci (outputs sync-on-green): Does not work - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Monitor shows nothing - cannot detect sync - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Limitation for Silicon Graphics, Sun, Mac systems - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Panel Quality Issues
- Claimed IPS but exhibits TN behavior - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Off-axis viewing causes significant brightness loss - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Colors remain stable off-axis (unlike typical TN color shift) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Problematic for filming/cameras due to narrow viewing angle - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Firmware Issues
- Gets glitchy/corrupted, needs power cycling - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Shows garbage on screen occasionally - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Black screen (no error message) for unsupported modes - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- No "not supported" message for some incompatible signals - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Mounting Options
- Removable stand with slot attachment - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Can wall-mount using slot with lag screws - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Stand has VESA mount holes - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Double-sided adhesive foam pad included for non-slip base - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Screen can be flipped for inverted mounting (under shelf) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]

#### Verdict
- Good for VGA use with DOS/vintage PCs - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Good for HDMI with RetroTink line-doubled output - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Poor for direct composite from 8-bit computers (no 240p) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Useless for component video (broken) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Useless for sync-on-green systems (Mac, SGI) - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
- Primary use case: VGA and HDMI - Source: [CluzXuInAqU]
