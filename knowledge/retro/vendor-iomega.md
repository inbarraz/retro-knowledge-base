## Platform-Specific Knowledge

### Iomega Clik / Pocket Zip Drive

#### Overview
- Released December 1998 as "Clik", renamed "Pocket Zip" in 2000 - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Renamed to avoid association with Zip drive "Click of Death" failures - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- 40MB micro-floppy drive in PCMCIA card form factor - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Actually contains spinning magnetic disc, not flash storage - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Disc shelf life advertised as 10 years - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Assembled in Japan - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### Specifications
- Capacity: 40MB (38.3MB formatted) - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Rotational speed: 2941 RPM - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Seek time: 38ms - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Transfer rate: 150-460KB/s actual (600KB/s theoretical) - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Interface: Shows up as IDE/ATAPI controller - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Uses non-standard I/O port 180h (not standard IDE 1F0h) - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### Original Pricing (1998)
- Drive + 1 disc retail package: $249 - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Individual 40MB discs: $9.99 each (in 10-pack) - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Comparable 64MB flash card cost $120-160 at the time - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### Compatibility
- Windows 95, 98, NT 4.0 officially supported - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Windows XP works (installs as standard IDE/ATAPI controller) - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Linux: May not work - shows as device but disc doesn't spin up - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Mac: PCMCIA version specified as not compatible - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### Operating Characteristics
- Makes beep sound when PCMCIA card inserted - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Spins up when disc accessed, spins down quickly to save battery - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Gets noticeably warm during operation (battery drain concern) - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Disc ejects by pushing in (click releases latch) - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### Disc Mechanism
- Disc is truly floppy (flexible magnetic media) - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Metal shutter protects disc surface - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Small latch releases when pushed down slightly - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Very thin pancake motor interfaces with disc - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### 100MB Version (Planned)
- Announced 2001, never released - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Would have required new drive hardware - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- 40MB discs would be readable on 100MB drive - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Planned to include DRM for music storage - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### Related Products
- Iomega Hip Zip: MP3 player using Clik discs - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Sensory Science Rave MP: Another Clik-based MP3 player - Source: [435G8badZhA]

#### Why It Failed
- Came out when flash storage capacity/price rapidly improving - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Moving parts = reliability concerns vs solid state - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Power consumption higher than flash cards - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Sales poor by September 2000, product dead by 2001-2002 - Source: [435G8badZhA]

## Technical Notes

### PCMCIA/CardBus Storage
- Compact Flash cards show up as standard IDE drives - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- IDE = ISA bus extension through ribbon cable - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- CF to IDE adapters are mostly passive (no ICs needed) - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Different PCMCIA cards may get different I/O port assignments - Source: [435G8badZhA]

### Performance Comparison (1999 Era)
- Click drive: 150-460KB/s - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Old Cisco CF card: Up to 900KB/s - Source: [435G8badZhA]
- Floppy drive: Much slower than Click - Source: [435G8badZhA]
